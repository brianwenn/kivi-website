Why your head quits before your body.

Understanding heat regulation in the sauna.

Your head dissipates up to 40% of your body's heat. In normal environments, this keeps you cool. In a sauna, it works against you.

Because heat rises, your head is exposed to the highest temperatures in the room. Without protection, your scalp, ears, and brain overheat rapidly - long before your body reaches its limit.

This is why most people leave the sauna feeling like they "couldn't take it anymore." It's not your body. It's your head.

Wool's cellular structure creates air pockets that slow heat transfer. Unlike synthetic materials, wool also wicks moisture away from the skin, preventing the "wet heat" effect that accelerates discomfort.

Care Guide

Your KIVI sauna hat is built to last hundreds of sessions. Proper care ensures it performs at its best for years to come.

Daily Care

After each sauna session, your hat needs minimal attention.

Immediate Post-Session

Shake out excess moisture. Hang in a well-ventilated area to air dry. Never leave damp in a bag or enclosed space. The hat should be completely dry before your next use.

Between Sessions

Store in a dry, ventilated space. Avoid plastic bags or airtight containers. A hook in your sauna or bathroom works well. Natural airflow prevents moisture buildup and maintains shape.

Cleaning Your Hat

Wool naturally resists odor and bacteria. Most users never need to wash their hat. Occasional spot cleaning is sufficient for long-term use.

Spot Cleaning

Use cool water and a small amount of mild, wool-safe soap. Gently dab the affected area - never rub aggressively. Rinse with cool water. Press (don't wring) to remove excess water. Air dry flat on a clean towel, away from direct heat or sunlight.

Deep Cleaning (Rarely Needed)

Fill a basin with cool water and a small amount of wool wash or gentle detergent. Submerge the hat and gently agitate for 1â€“2 minutes. Let it soak for 10 minutes maximum. Rinse thoroughly with cool water until soap is completely removed. Press gently to remove water - never twist or wring. Reshape while damp. Lay flat on a clean, dry towel. Allow to air dry completely (24â€“48 hours). Never use a dryer, radiator, or direct heat source.

Do

  • Air dry after every use
  • Store in a ventilated area
  • Use cool water for cleaning
  • Use wool-safe or gentle soap
  • Reshape while damp

  • Machine wash

Longevity & Maintenance

Normal Wear

Some pilling is natural with wool products. Gently remove pills with a fabric shaver or sweater comb. Do not pull pills by hand - this can damage fibers. Slight stretching over time is normal. Reshape while damp if needed.

Expected Lifespan

With proper care, your KIVI hat will last 200â€“500 sessions. Heavy use (daily sessions) may show wear after 1â€“2 years. Occasional use (weekly sessions) can last 5+ years.

When to Replace

Significant thinning of the wool. Loss of structural integrity (won't hold shape). Damage that affects heat protection. Normal wear and tear is covered under our quality standards.

Troubleshooting

Hat smells after use

Ensure complete drying after each session. Air out in fresh air for several hours. Spot clean if needed. Odor-resistant wool rarely requires deep cleaning.

Hat has lost its shape

Dampen with cool water. Reshape by hand while damp. Lay flat to dry on a clean towel. Avoid over-stretching during reshaping.

Wool feels rough or stiff

This can happen after improper washing. Rewet with cool water and a small amount of wool conditioner. Gently work through fibers. Rinse and air dry. Prevention: Always use cool water and wool-safe products.

Travel & Storage

Traveling with Your Hat

Ensure hat is completely dry before packing. Pack in a breathable bag or leave uncovered in luggage. Avoid crushing under heavy items. Reshape upon arrival if needed.

Long-Term Storage

If storing for months, ensure hat is completely clean and dry. Store in a breathable cotton or linen bag. Keep in a cool, dry place. Check periodically for moths (rare, but possible with natural wool).
